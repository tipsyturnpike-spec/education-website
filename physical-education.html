Physical Education

Moving Bodies, Thinking Minds

Physical education isn't a break from learning - it's a different way of learning. Every sport, game, and outdoor activity is packed with math, science, strategy, and social studies. We just make it feel like play.

Team Sports: Math in Motion

Every sport is geometry and physics in action. Students learn by playing, not by sitting.

Elementary: Learning Through Play

üßÆ Math Integration: How hard do you need to kick to reach the outfield? That's force = mass √ó acceleration. Students feel it before they name it.

Relay Races

Looks like: Running and having fun

üßÆ Math Integration: Calculate average speed. If you ran 50 meters in 10 seconds, how fast is that in miles per hour? Real data collection.

Basketball Basics

Looks like: Shooting hoops

Actually learning: Angles, force, trajectory

üßÆ Math Integration: The angle of the shot determines success. Students experiment: too flat? too high? They're learning quadratic equations through their bodies.
üìä

Stats Kids Love

Every game becomes a math lesson. Students track:

  • How many throws until you hit the target? (Probability)
  • What's your personal best time? (Data tracking)
  • How many points did your team score? (Addition)
Teacher Tip: Have students keep "sports journals" with simple stats. They're doing math without worksheets.

  • Create graphs showing improvement
  • Calculate averages, medians, modes
  • Predict outcomes based on past data
üî¨ Science Connection: Why does a curveball curve? That's physics - air pressure, spin, velocity. Students experience it before they learn the formula.
